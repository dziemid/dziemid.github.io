Feel Good About Public Speaking - Thanks to Virtual Reality

Feb 3rd, 2016

Free Google Cardboard Public Speaking app

Well known fact – many of us are afraid of speaking in front of an audience.

Actually, many people put fear of public speaking in front of things like death – sounds pretty serious :–)

Did you know, that practicing in front of virtual audience can remove this fear? It may not be obvious at first, but speaking in front of the virtual audience can boost your confidence in a real world.

There is some research to back up this statement, for example:

Higher perceived audience interest (in VR) increases self-rating and reduces public speaking anxiety.

Public Speaking in Virtual Reality: Facing an Audience of Avatars www0.cs.ucl.ac.uk/staff/Papers/…

or Virtual Reality Therapy: An Effective Treatment for Psychological Disorders.

Background

Few years ago in Toastmasters, I’ve learned about the technique that helps me focus on the audience when practicing speech at home.

It involves finding some old magazines with people on the cover and setting them up around the room, so that you are facing a “virtual audience”. It’s not very responsive group of folks, but very patient :–) Anyway, it help with things like maintaining an eye contact.

Another technique I’ve learnt, was “positive visualization”. Basically, you imagine that you are delivering your speech and the audience is smiling and happy. This in turn, makes you more relaxed and confident when you get to deliver your speech for real.

Solution with Google Cardboard

Recently I’ve combined what I’ve learnt in Toastmasters with the research I’ve found on-line. “Public Speaking for Cardboard VR” was born :–)

In the app, you are standing in front of the very receptive and supportive audience. They look cartoonish which will have a relaxing effect on you.

The app helps you learn to maintain an eye contact with members of the audience, by rewarding you every time you do it. Look one of the avatars in the eyes, and he will give you a standing ovation.

Last but not least, because you will be presumably wearing VR goggles, there is no way to rely on notes. By mastering your speech by heart you will be able to keep your audience engaged by maintaining an eye contact throughout the speech.
